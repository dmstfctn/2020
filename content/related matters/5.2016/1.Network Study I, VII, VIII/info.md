NETWORK STUDY I, Ⅶ & Ⅷ are audiovisual pieces exploring urban network infrastructure. Each reproduces the activity of wireless networks by translating captured data into sound, text, pictograms&#8196;and&#8196;warping&#8196;satellite&#8196;imagery.

NETWORK STUDY I explores the London Internet Exchange area of the London Docklands, inhabited by buildings with imposing grey exteriors,<span class="dc-hide-on-small-landscape"> surrounded by metal fencing and security cameras,</span> where 600 operators&#8196;connect&#8196;to&#8196;exchange&#8196;traffic.<COLBREAK><span class="dc-hide-on-small-landscape"> In its proximity is Canary Wharf, a key European financial hub, thriving on the network performance and scalability offered by the London Internet Exchange.</span>

NETWORK STUDY Ⅶ explores an area of the Vatican City housing the Governorate's Department of Telecommunication and the Vatican Radio<span class="dc-hide-on-large">.</span><span class="dc-hide-on-small-landscape">; having supported Marconi's research into radio transmissions in the 1930s, the Vatican was an early adopter of a stable shortwave transmission system. Ever since,<COLBREAK> the Vatican has maintained a rich infrastructure of networks for telecommunications and radio propaganda.</span>
